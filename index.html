What is Goodreads?

Problem


Goodreads is a popular website that is used by authors and readers to organize, discover, and post books. Despite the popularity the webiste is sorely outdated and has many frustrating UI elements that hinder the user experience. Once such issue is how books are shared with friends. Naturally, you want to be able to connect with friends, see their activity, and read their reviews. However, the most basic function of "sharing" is odd. The app forces you to go through multiple through third-party platforms like messages only to then send a basic url that the user has to copy themselves onto a browser. For both sender and reciever, it is a convulated process so many don't use this feature.


Example below:

Step 1: Click share icon (far right)

Step 2: Copy URL

After copying the URL, the user will have to manually select their messaging app to then type in their friend's name. However, some people you meet online you might not be as personal with. And the other options are also lacking in versatility.

Step 3: The receiver clicks the URL and gets redirected

The reciever is redirected back to goodreads, however, if they are not signed into their phone or don't have the app downloaded. They are sent to a browser and will be forced to sign in from there.

Solution


My solution is to create a more streamlined method of sharing, for instance, once you select your book and click share your friend's page will show up and allow you select which friend to send it to or there is a send all option as well. Once sent, the book will be placed into the friend's book recommendation shelf generated by the webiste. For context, a shelf in goodreads is how a users books are organized. There are three that are part of the app which is read, want to read, and currently reading; however, users can create custom shelves as well. It would make sense to integrate the sharing into a feature that already exists in the app.
